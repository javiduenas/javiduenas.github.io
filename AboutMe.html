 PHILOSOPHY OF TEACHING & LEARNING

Growth cannot happen without change, and learning cannot happen without a personal commitment to change.
 
The way this change manifests itself and the degree of change manifested by a learner can be influenced by many factors, but the most important is a sincere commitment to change. The second is humility. Learning depends on the ability to accept mistakes, examine those mistakes, and grow because of them.

Although learning can sometimes feel like a solitary activity, especially in online environments, it is a fundamentally social activity, transferring ideas, emotions, and experiences through familial and societal networks usin the earliest form of educational technology known to man: Language.
